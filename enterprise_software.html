CRM

CRM or Customer Relationship Management is a system for managing a company’s interactions with current and future customers. It often involves using technology to organize, automate and synchronize sales, marketing, customer service, and technical support.
CRM software allows a business build a relationship with customers by automating communications with them. It gives customers the feeling of personal services. A good CRM system gathers info from lots of sources across the business and beyond. The business gets insights into how customers feel and what they are saying about the business.
Moving CRM to the “cloud” has dramatically increased its usefulness. It is argued “Cloud-based CRM systems such as Salesforce mean every user has the same Info, all the time. Your sales force out on the road can check data, update it instantly after a meeting or work from anywhere. The same info is available to anyone who needs it, from the sales team to the customer service representative”.
-Salesforce

Online Payment Services

All small business owners rely on getting paid by their customers on time and in full in order to keep up with the cost of operating the business. For these businesses, there are many online payment options to uses such as:

Credit cards
Credit cards are still the most popular way to pay for goods and services online.

PayPal
PayPal provides an all-in-one online payment solution that makes transactions easy. The service accepts Visa, Mastercard, Discover and American Express credit cards to create the transaction with the customer.

Sum Up

Sum up is like a credit card scanner but this service is through a phone. On the phone you will have the card you want to make a payment with, you will then enter the amount on the app then scan and pay.

SCM

SCM or Supply Chain Management Software involves the oversight of materials, info and finances as they move in a process from supplier to manufacturer to wholesaler to retailer to consumer, coordination and integration with in workforce, product flow which is the movement of goods from supplier to customer, information flow and financial flow.
