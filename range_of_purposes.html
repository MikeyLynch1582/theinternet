There are range of many different purposes on the internet to use. These are communication, research, collaboration and e-commerce. In this section we will investigate voice and written communication under the communication heading, information search under research heading, file downloading and sharing under collaboration heading and e-commerce under the e-commerce heading.

Communication

  • Voice Communication

We have three different uses of the internet in voice communication. These are audio conferencing, video conferencing and VoIP.

  • Audio Conferencing

Audio conferencing is a connection between two computers that requires participants to have a microphone and speakers. Using an instant messaging program, such as America Online Messenger (AOL), or an audio-conferencing software, such as Skype or Zoom, users speak into a microphone and the dialogue is heard through the speakers of the other users.

  • Video/Web Conferencing

Video Conferencing works much like audio conferencing. The differences are that the users are able to see each other and for this a webcam is needed by all parties. Depending upon the service used, multiple people can see and be seen at the same time. Examples of this are; Cisco, Microsoft Teams and Zoom.

  • VoIP

Video over Internet Protocol (VoIP), is phone service through an internet connection. Users are given an adapter and a unique phone number. The user can then send and receive phone calls using a computer, a VoIP phone or a traditional phone, to and from other people, whether or not they use a VoIP connection themselves. Examples of this are; Blueface and Skype.

  • Written Communication

There are four uses of written communication on the internet. These are: E-mail, Instant Messaging, IRC and SMS + Wireless communications.

  • Email

E-mail is a transmission of messages over communications networks. The messages can be notes entered from a keyboard or electronic files stored on disk. Most mainframes, minicomputers and computer networks have an e-mail system. Some e-mail systems are confined to a single computer system or network, but others have gateways to other computer systems, enabling you to send e-mail anywhere in the world.
Using an email client (software such as Microsoft Outlook and Eudora), you can compose an e-mail message and send it to another person anywhere, as long as you know the recipient e-mail address. I usually only takes a few seconds for the email to arrive at the destination.
one of the downfalls of email is spam. Spam is a form of junk mail which normally represents a product on sale, and this email is normally sent in thousand if not millions. Sometimes, these can contain viruses which can affect your computer. For this reason, you should invest in anti-virus software.

  • Instant Messaging

One of the fastest growing forms of internet communication is instant messaging. Instant messaging is a text-based computer text chain between two or more people. Instant messaging systems normally notify you whenever somebody contacts you.
One reason the instant messaging has become so popular is its real-time nature. Unlike e-mail, where you will wait for the person to check the email and send a reply, if a person you want to reach is online and available in your contacts, your message appears instantly inÂ  a window on their screen.
While instant messaging is used by millions of internet users to contact family and friends, it’s also growing in popularity in the business world. Employees of a company can have instant access to managers and co-workers in different offices or locations and can eliminate the need to place phone calls when info is required immediately.

  • IRC

Internet Relay Chat (IRC), is a multi-user chat system that allows people to gather on “channels” or “rooms” to talk in groups or in private. IRC is based on a client/server model. That is, to join an IRC discussion, you need an IRC client and internet access. The IRC client is a program that runs on your computer and sends and receives messages to and from an IRC server. The IRC server, in turn, is responsible for making sure that all messages are broadcasted to everyone in the discussion. With many IRC clients you can easily create your own chatroom and invite others to join your channel. You can also protect channels by adding a password to the chatroom.

  • SMS & Wireless Communications

Short message service (SMS), is a global wireless service that enables the transmission of alphanumeric messages between mobile subscribers and external systems such as e-mail, paging and voice-mail systems. Messages can be no longer than 160 alpha-numeric characters and must contain no images or graphics. Once a message is sent, it is received by a Short Message Service Centre, which must then get it to the appropriate mobile device or system. As wireless services evolved, Multimedia Messaging Service was introduced and provided a way to send messages comprising a combination of text, sounds, images and video to multimedia messaging service users.

Research

There are many building blocks to make up the research aspect of the internet. These all interlink with each other to help users find info to research up on. Some of the cogs that help the research machine are web browsers, URL, hyperlinks

The internet is made up of web browsers that make it easier to access information that you need to find. Some of the most popular ones are, Firefox, Google, Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge and Internet Explorer Within these browsers we can type in keywords into a search bar to find relevant websites or we can type in URL’s that we know will bring us to a certain website. Uniform Resource Locator (URL) is the global address of documents and other resources on the World Wide Web. The first part of the address is called a protocol identifier and it indicates what protocol to use, and the second part is called a resource name and it specifies the IP address or the domain name where the resource is located. The protocol identifier and the resource name are separated by a colon and two forward slashes.

If you look up information through keywords the search will bring up hyperlinks. Hyperlinks are an element in an electronic document that links to another place in the same document or to an entirely different document. Typically, you click on the hyperlink to follow the link.

All these elements make it easier for the user to find the information they need.

Collaboration

There are two different types of collaboration to use on the internet. These are File Sharing, File Downloading.

 

  • File sharing

File sharing is the public or private sharing of computer data or space in a network with various levels of access. While files can easily be shared outside the network, the term file sharing almost always means sharing files in a network, even if in small local area network.
File sharing allows several people to use the same file or file by some combination of being able to read or view it, write to or modify it, copy it, or print it. Typically, a file sharing system has one or more administrators. Users may all have the same or may have different levels of access privilege. File sharing can also mean having an allocated amount of personal file storage in a common file system.

  • File downloading
  • Peer-To-Peer Downloading

Users can use software that connects in to a peer-to-peer network to search for shared files on the computers of other users connected to the network. Files of interest can then be downloaded directly from other users on the network. Typically, large files are broken down into smaller chunks, which mat be obtained from multiple peers an then reassembled by the downloader. This is done while the peer is simultaneously uploading the chunks it already has to other peers.

  • File Hosting Services

File hosting services are a simple alternative to peer-to-peer software. These are sometimes used together with internet collaboration tools such as email, forums, blogs, or any other medium in which links to direct downloads from file hosting service can be embedded. These sites typically host files so that others can download them.

  • File Sync and Sharing

Cloud-based file syncing and sharing services implement automated file transfers by updating files from dedicated sharing directory on each user’s networked devices.
Files placed in this folder also are typically accessible through a website and mobile app, and can be easily shared with other users for viewing or collaboration.

E-Commerce

E-Commerce is the buying and selling of goods and services, or the transmitting of funds or data, over an electronic network, primarily the internet. These business transactions occur either as business-to-business, business-to-consumer or consumer-to-business.
